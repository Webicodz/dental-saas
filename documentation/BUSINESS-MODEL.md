## Common Objections & Responses

### "It's too expensive"

**Response:**  
"I understand. Let's look at the ROI. If you have 20 appointments per week and reduce no-shows by just 25%, that's 5 extra appointments. At $150 per appointment, that's $750/week or $39,000/year. The system pays for itself in less than 5 months. Plus, your staff saves hours daily on manual scheduling and reminders."

**Alternative:**  
Offer payment plan: $5,000 down, $5,000 in 30 days, $2,999 in 60 days.

### "We're using [Current System]"

**Response:**  
"That's great! What do you like most about it? And if you could change one thing, what would it be?"

Then focus on:
- AI features they don't have
- Cost comparison (often cheaper long-term)
- Source code access for customization
- Self-hosted (they control their data)

### "What about the monthly API costs?"

**Response:**  
"Great question! The beauty is transparency - you pay providers directly, no markup. For a typical practice, it's $75-150/month. But here's the key: the app tracks every penny in real-time. If chatbot costs $50 but books 20 extra appointments worth $3,000, that's incredible ROI. Plus, you can turn features on/off anytime to control costs."

### "We need to think about it"

**Response:**  
"Absolutely, this is an important decision. To help you think through it, what specific questions or concerns do you have? I'd love to address them now while we're together."

Or offer:  
"How about a 30-day trial installation? Try it risk-free, then decide."

### "Can we see it working at another clinic?"

**Response:**  
"Yes! I can connect you with Dr. [Reference] at [Clinic]. They've been using it for [X] months. Would you like their contact, or should I arrange a call?"

(Build your reference list early!)
